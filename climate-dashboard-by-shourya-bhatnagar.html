Key Findings

04 / 04
01

China and the United States together account for more than half of top-20 CO₂ emissions in 2023, though their trajectories diverge sharply — China's output has risen over 5× since 1990, while the U.S. has stayed roughly flat.

02

The last decade (2014–2023) holds nine of the ten warmest years on record in this dataset, consistent with an accelerating warming trend since the 1980s.

03

Sea level has risen at a non-constant rate — the annual pace in the most recent decade is noticeably higher than in the 1990s, pointing to acceleration rather than a steady linear trend.

04

Several European economies (Germany, UK, France, Italy) show sustained emissions declines since 1990, while emerging economies (India, Indonesia, Iran, Turkey) show the steepest growth — a regional decoupling pattern.

Data is compiled and approximated for demonstration purposes from patterns consistent with public sources — NASA GISS (temperature), NOAA (sea level), and Our World in Data / Global Carbon Project (CO₂ emissions). Figures are illustrative rather than exact real-time values. Built with Pandas/NumPy-style preprocessing and Plotly.js.
